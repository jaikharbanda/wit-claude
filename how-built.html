How this workshop
built itself

Every deck, run sheet and web page in this series was made with Claude Cowork, by someone who is completely non-technical. Here's how, from nothing to everything you've seen.

Two weeks ago I did a session with Women in Tech on an intro to Claude. I'm doing session 3 tomorrow, 2:00–4:30. The organiser shared a guide for the curriculum. Help me plan the session, so we have enough material for the full 2.5 hours. When that's done we'll pull together all the assets.

1 · Week 2 was missed. Merge it into week 3?
2 · Hands-on with a free-plan fallback, or demo-led?
3 · Which assets: deck, hub update, run sheet, demo pack?
4 · The guide plugs a paid dictation tool. Include it?

Claude's first move wasn't to build. It interviewed me. Week 1's habit, doing production work.

Watch them change.

Act 4 · The rules, written once

A skill: my standards, saved as a file.

# boom-event-kit
Google Slides, always.  Brand is law.
No em dashes, anywhere.
A typed URL next to every QR (laptops can't scan).
Every QR gets decode-verified in code before shipping.
Time-boxes must sum.  The close is scripted, never improvised.
Nothing real on the projector.

A template teaches Claude a structure. A skill teaches it a job. Every week's kit now follows these rules without me repeating them.

Act 5 · Each week fed the next

Nothing was thrown away. It compounded.

W1 · 20 Jun

The pattern is born

Deck, run sheet, a one-page hub, and giveaway #1: the anti-AI writing style skill. Fed forward: that skill became a file in everyone's ABOUT ME folder two weeks later.

W2 · 27 Jun

Postponed, not lost

The session didn't happen. In chat, we folded its whole curriculum into week 3: that was the first question Claude asked in Act 1. The plan absorbed a week; no content died.

W3 · 4 Jul

The kit grows a memory

Same rules, new deck. The hub gained the folder tree, Prompts 1–5 and giveaway #2: the about-me interview. Midweek: a step-by-step guide grown from the room's questions, then the lecture videos. Your questions literally became the website.

W4 · 11 Jul

Tabs, so history stays live

The hub grew tabs: weeks 1, 3 and 4 side by side, two more skill giveaways, today's build prompt, and this page. Everything from week 1 is still up, still used.

One website. 26 commits in 21 days. The deck and this page were rebuilt from the same rules every week; the hub just kept growing.

Act 7 · What surprised me?

Three things I didn't expect.

1

It checks its own work

A fresh pair of AI eyes reviewed every rendered slide and caught layout problems I'd stopped seeing.

2

It edits around me

When I hand-tweaked slides, it worked around my changes instead of overwriting them.

3

Failure made it better

The week the projector refused my laptop, we rebuilt the plan so any PC in the room needs exactly one URL. You're looking at it.

The point

I went from completely non-technical to building all of this on my own.

There is nothing here you can't do after today.
